# VC Outline

## A) Industry Thesis Template (Slides 1–5)
Use this exact structure every time. Swap in your industry specific data, companies, and numbers.

## B) Cover Header

Section Title: Strategies, and Market Maps

Industry #: Industry X, [Industry Name] ([Short descriptor], ex B2B Fintech)

## C) Slide 1: Emerging Trends of Interest

Purpose: Prove the market is shifting now, and why it matters.

Layout: 3 bullets, each with a bold label + 1–2 lines.

- The $X Shift
  - One big market level metric with a date attached.
  - Format: “$X market / $X volume / X% CAGR by YYYY” + source.
- The Business Model Multiplier
  - How this trend increases ARPU, retention, margins, or distribution.
  - Format: “Revenue per customer increases X to Y when doing Z.”
- Regulatory / Platform / Technical Inflection
  - The enabling change. Law, standard, API, hardware, cost curve, behavior.
  - Format: “New rule/standard reduces friction for X.”

Inputs to fill: market size projection, value driver mechanism, inflection catalyst, citations.

## D) Slide 2: Taxonomy and Landscape

Purpose: Give a clean map of the ecosystem so you can filter companies fast.

Layout: 3 bullets with a simple stack model.

- The Core
  - The foundational infrastructure, system of record, or primitive.
- The Layer
  - Orchestration, tooling, analytics, compliance, automation, AI.
  - Include 1 quant claim if available.
- The Edge
  - Distribution surfaces, workflow owners, vertical platforms, end users.

Optional add on: 1 line that says “Where value accrues” or “Where moats form.”

Inputs to fill: 3 part stack definition, value capture hypothesis.

## E) Slide 3: Macro Market Thesis

Purpose: State your POV and where the venture returns are.

Layout: 3 bullets.

- Focus
  - The sub segment you actually care about.
  - Ex: “Middle market infrastructure”, “SMB distribution”, “enterprise compliance”.
- The Opinion
  - A sharp take with contrast.
  - Format: “X is commoditizing, alpha is in Y.”
- Thought Leadership Hook
  - A 2–5 word phrase that becomes the theme.
  - Then one sentence that defines it.
  - Ex: “Silent Finance”, “Zero touch ops”, “Autonomous compliance”.

Inputs to fill: your wedge, your contrarian take, your thematic hook.

## F) Slide 4: Company Profile Filters

Purpose: Define what qualifies as a “venture scale winner” in this space.

Layout: 3 bullets. Make them measurable.

- Metrics
  - Traction and growth thresholds.
  - Ex: “30% MoM”, “$X ARR”, “NRR > 130%”.
- Stickiness
  - Integration depth, switching costs, workflow embed, data moat.
- Stage
  - Where you invest, plus unit economics.
  - Ex: “Seed/Series A”, “LTV/CAC 3:1”, “payback < 12 months”.

Inputs to fill: growth bar, retention bar, integration wedge, stage, unit economics.

## G) Slide 5: Candidate Companies

Purpose: Show you know the landscape and have targets.

Layout: 3 bullets, each one line, label + short descriptor.

- Company 1: Category leader in [core].
- Company 2: Best in class [layer].
- Company 3: Incumbent benchmark / distribution anchor / edge player.

Rule: These 3 should map to Core, Layer, Edge whenever possible.

Inputs to fill: 2–5 candidate list, your top 3, why each matters.
